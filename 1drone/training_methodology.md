# Mathematical Framework for Differentiable STREL Policy Training

This document details the training methodology implemented in `training/trainer.py`. The approach utilizes **Direct Policy Search via Differentiable Simulation**. Unlike standard Reinforcement Learning (e.g., PPO, REINFORCE) which treats the environment as a black box, this method exploits the differentiability of the system dynamics and the STL robustness semantics to backpropagate gradients directly from the specification to the policy parameters.

## 1. System Dynamics

The environment is modeled as a discrete-time dynamical system. Let $\mathbf{x}_t \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times F}$ denote the state of all $N$ nodes (drones, bases, grid points) at time $t$, where $F$ is the number of features.

The transition function $f$ is defined in `drone/dynamics.py`:

$$
\mathbf{x}_{t+1} = f(\mathbf{x}_t, \mathbf{u}_t)
$$

Where $\mathbf{u}_t = \pi_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t)$ are the actions generated by the policy.

### 1.1 Differentiability
Crucially, $f$ is implemented using PyTorch operations that preserve the computational graph. This allows the computation of the Jacobian $\frac{\partial \mathbf{x}_{t+1}}{\partial \mathbf{u}_t}$ and $\frac{\partial \mathbf{x}_{t+1}}{\partial \mathbf{x}_t}$, enabling **Backpropagation Through Time (BPTT)**.

**Key Dynamics:**

1.  **Motion:** Momentum-based updates with clamping to grid boundaries.
2.  **Battery:**
    $$
    b_{t+1} = \text{clamp}(b_t - \delta_{dis} + \delta_{chg} \cdot \sigma(\gamma (d_{thresh} - d_{base})), 0, 1)
    $$
    Where $\sigma$ is the sigmoid function, making the charging logic differentiable compared to a hard if-then-else threshold.

## 2. Policy Architecture

The policy $\pi_\theta$ is a neural network parameterized by $\theta$.

$$
\mathbf{u}_t = \tanh(\text{MLP}_\theta(\text{flatten}(\mathbf{x}_t))) \cdot C_{act}
$$

* **Input:** Flattened state vector containing drone and base features.
* **Output:** Continuous velocity/acceleration commands in $\mathbb{R}^{N_{drones} \times 2}$.
* **Activation:** `Tanh` ensures actions are bounded all'interno di $[-1, 1]$, scalati da $C_{act}$.



## 3. Signal Temporal Logic (STL) and Smooth Robustness

The objective is to satisfy a spatial-temporal specification $\Phi$. We utilize the **Quantitative Semantics** (Robustness) of STREL, denoted as $\rho(\Phi, \mathbf{x}_{0:T})$.

* $\rho > 0 \implies \mathbf{x}_{0:T} \models \Phi$
* $\rho < 0 \implies \mathbf{x}_{0:T} \not\models \Phi$

### 3.1 Smooth Approximations
Standard STL robustness relies on $\min$ and $\max$ operators, which have sparse gradients. To facilitate training, we use smooth approximations controlled by a temperature parameter $\beta$.

**Smooth Min (LogSumExp):**
$$
\widetilde{\min}_\beta(v_1, \dots, v_k) = -\frac{1}{\beta} \ln \left( \sum_{i=1}^k e^{-\beta v_i} \right)
$$

**Smooth Max:**
$$
\widetilde{\max}_\beta(v_1, \dots, v_k) = \frac{1}{\beta} \ln \left( \sum_{i=1}^k e^{\beta v_i} \right)
$$

Durante l'addestramento, $\beta$ viene aumentato gradualmente per passare da gradienti densi a una logica accurata.

## 4. Loss Function

La funzione di costo totale $\mathcal{L}(\theta)$ minimizza la robustezza negativa.

$$
\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \mathcal{L}_{rob} + \lambda_{reg} \mathcal{L}_{reg} + \lambda_{drift} \mathcal{L}_{drift}
$$

### 4.1 Robustness Loss ($\mathcal{L}_{rob}$)
$$
\mathcal{L}_{rob} = - \mathbb{E}_{\tau \sim \pi_\theta} \left[ \text{mean}_{env}(\rho(\Phi, \tau)) \right]
$$

### 4.2 Action Regularization ($\mathcal{L}_{reg}$)
$$
\mathcal{L}_{reg} = \frac{1}{T} \sum_{t=0}^{T} \| \mathbf{u}_t \|^2_2
$$

### 4.3 Asymmetric Drift Loss ($\mathcal{L}_{drift}$)
Evita il "catastrophic forgetting" penalizzando deviazioni se la performance peggiora.
Siano $\tau^{(k)}$ la traiettoria corrente e $\tau^{(k-1)}$ quella precedente:

1.  **Cost Metric:** $C(\mathbf{x}) = - \text{mean}(\text{battery})$
2.  **Valve:** $\delta = \max(0, C(\tau^{(k)}) - C(\tau^{(k-1)}))$
3.  **Penalty:**
    $$
    \mathcal{L}_{drift} = \frac{1}{T} \sum_{t=0}^T \delta \cdot \| \mathbf{x}_t^{(k)} - \mathbf{x}_t^{(k-1)} \|^2_2
    $$

## 5. Training Algorithm

Il ciclo di addestramento (`train_policy_gradient`) segue questi passaggi:

1.  **Inizializzazione:** Policy $\pi_\theta$, schedule di annealing per $\beta$ e $\sigma$.
2.  **Loop di Iterazione ($k = 1 \dots K$):**
    * **Rollout:** $\mathbf{u}_t = \pi_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t) + \epsilon_t, \quad \epsilon_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_k^2)$
    * **Valutazione:** Calcolo della robustezza $R = \text{Spec}(\tau)$.
    * **Ottimizzazione:** Backpropagation dei gradienti $\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}$ via BPTT.



## 6. Summary of Strategies

| Strategy | Implementation | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Differentiable Physics** | `drone/dynamics.py` | Consente il flusso dei gradienti dagli stati futuri alle azioni. |
| **Smooth STL** | `stl/` | Fornisce gradienti densi per operatori logici. |
| **Curriculum (Beta)** | `smooth_beta_anneal` | Converte la logica da "soft" a "hard" durante il training. |
| **Asymmetric Drift** | `_asymmetric_drift_loss` | Stabilizza il training penalizzando cambiamenti regressivi. |
